    Why it matters

    2PC guarantees atomic outcome

    Two-phase commit is an atomic commit protocol: all participants should reach the same final decision. The tradeoff is blocking behavior. After a participant votes ACK and enters the prepared state, it may have to wait for the coordinator's final COMMIT or ABORT decision.

    Mapped to this repo: Coordinator.py sends PREPARE, waits for two ACK messages, then broadcasts COMMIT; if any vote fails, it broadcasts ABORT.

    References

    The model is based on the classic two-phase commit protocol used in distributed transaction systems. It illustrates the prepare/vote phase, the final decision phase, all-or-nothing commit behavior, and the blocking risk when a coordinator or participant fails mid-protocol.
